Insurance Factor analysis

The objective of this EDA and Regression analysis is to find a relationship between unorthodox factors of insurance industry and also to form a tailor made premium logic for each individual by using those factors.

Background:

Insurance industry workflow crosses through Federal/ State law, attorneys, product owners, underwriters, actuary, business, IT, brokers, agents and finally reaches into users hands.

The premium for each individual is calculated based on Tier, Credits, surcharges, Endorsements, commissions, Taxes, Capping and stabilization factors.

Traditional premium deriving logic is based on orthodox factor distribution and tier separation logic. But by using EDA and Machine learning techniques we can derive a tailor made premium calculation logic for users.

Content:

The data set is present in the below path. https://github.com/stedy/Machine-Learning-with-R-datasets

Original source: unknown

Columns - age: age of primary beneficiary

sex: insurance contractor gender, female, male

bmi: Body mass index, providing an understanding of body, weights that are relatively high or low relative to height, objective index of body weight (kg / m ^ 2) using the ratio of height to weight, ideally 18.5 to 24.9

children: Number of children covered by health insurance / Number of dependents smoker: Smoking region: the beneficiarys residential area in the US, northeast, southeast, southwest, northwest.

charges: Individual medical costs billed by health insurance

Purpose:

Numerous people in different hierarchy are working together every month in an insurance company to derive a new factor or logic to modify the premium, hence to explore the unexplored factors so far to derive a precise and profitable premium calculation logic is the purpose of this EDA and model.

pre_charges <- function(m, a, b, c){
pre_new <- predict(m, data.frame(age=a, bmi=b, children=c))
msg <- paste("age: ",a,", bmi: ",b,", children: ",c," ==> Expect Charges: $",round(pre_new),sep="")
print(msg)
}

Analysis Conclusion:

By predicting health care charges using linear regression methods, it is possible to impose different insurance premiums depending on the charges.

Obesity increases health care costs by $865, and smoking increases health care costs by $13,386. But, when both components are applied, (if smoking and obesity are together), It can be expected that medical expenses will increase the most with $19,329.

From the above observation we can formulate a new logic or tier to calculate premium for those who fall under the above category which will become a profitable approach with respective to insurance company. And will become a tailor-made insurance policy to user as well.
